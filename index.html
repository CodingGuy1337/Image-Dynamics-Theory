From Knowing to Understanding

Many modern systems know a lot and still fail at coherent prediction, causal stability, and adaptive planning. IDT proposes a simple center of gravity: a compact latent state Z, refined through an objective that measures mismatch between expectation and experience, under constraints, across time.

latent state Z energy or error prediction coherence goal shaped inference planning and control

IDT does not claim existing frameworks are identical. It claims they share a repeatable motif: latent state dynamics shaped by an objective, expressed through different vocabularies.

Core Claim

Representation
Cognition centers on a compact latent state Z encoding what the system believes is happening.
Dynamics
Thought is the iterative update of Z to reduce an objective under inputs and goals.
Understanding
Emerges when Z supports prediction, counterfactual simulation, and reliable action selection.
Bridge
A shared pattern across learning, inference, and control, written in different languages.
Plain reading: Z is the internal world summary, and descent is the mechanism that refines it until it is coherent enough to act.

Definitions

IDT becomes real only when the terms are explicit and measurable.

Y(t)
Observations at time t.
Z(t)
Latent state, the belief and world summary.
A(t)
Action or output that can alter future observations.
G(t)
Goals or constraints that shape what coherence means.
E(Z; Y, G)
Objective that measures mismatch, uncertainty, or goal distance.
Descent
An update rule that reduces E by adjusting Z, sometimes also A.
Quiet constraint: every term should have at least one concrete operationalization, even if it is imperfect.

Two Loops

A clean separation that prevents confusion and forces the theory to be implementable.

Inference time
Parameters are fixed. Z is refined by descent to reduce mismatch under the current observations and goals. This is the inner loop that corresponds to thought.
Learning time
Parameters change across experiences. The system updates the models that shape energy, prediction, and policy. This is the outer loop that sculpts the landscape thought moves through.
Interpretation: thought is not gradient descent on parameters. Thought is descent in state space under fixed parameters, plus optional action selection.

Math and Code

Minimal skeleton. Make it concrete and the rest becomes testable. 

# Signals
#   Y(t) observations
#   Z(t) latent state
#   G(t) goals or constraints

# Models
#   g_phi predictor or decoder
#   goal_distance constraint term
#   pi policy

Y_hat = g_phi(Z)

E(Z; Y, G) = ||Y - Y_hat||^2 + lambda * goal_distance(Z, G)

# Inference time inner loop thought update
Z = Z - alpha * dE_dZ

# Optional action selection
A = pi(Z, G)
Inference time: repeat the Z update until the objective stabilizes or time runs out.
Learning time: update parameters of g_phi and pi across many episodes, using the errors produced by the inner loop.

Predictions

Replace these with claims you are willing to be wrong about.

  • Explicit Z update loops improve robustness under distribution shift.
  • Difficult cognition corresponds to slower convergence or competing basins in the objective.
  • Counterfactual rollouts from Z predict outcomes better than surface heuristics.
  • Changing goals reshapes the descent path under the same observations.
  • Precision weighting predicts when attention amplifies or suppresses updates to Z.

Implementation Directions

Suggestions, not commandments. The point is to make the theory buildable.

Reflection head
Add a module that summarizes an episode and updates constraints or priors, separate from the moment to moment inference loop.
Contrastive structure
Use contrastive objectives to encourage Z to separate stable factors from incidental noise across contexts.
Attribution logging
Log which terms dominated E during descent, so you can audit what the system is responding to.
Two speed memory
Maintain a fast short term state for immediate coherence and a slower contextual state that shifts across episodes.
Goal modulation
Implement G as explicit constraints that reshape descent, not as vague prompting that disappears into tokens.
Diagnostics
Track convergence rate, basin switching events, and residual mismatch so prediction becomes measurable, not rhetorical.
Outcome: a system that can settle into a coherent internal state, explain what forced the settling, and then choose actions that preserve coherence under uncertainty.
