Overview
A mind as an image that moves.

Image Dynamics Theory (IDT) treats cognition as the evolution of a structured latent “image” under constraint minimization. Perception, prediction, action, goals, and reflection are different slices through the same underlying geometry.

Core claims

The mind maintains a structured internal state Z. Each moment updates Z by reducing a weighted sum of constraint errors: match current input, predict next input, keep intentions coherent, and keep internal relationships consistent. “Slices” are projections of Z that dominate awareness. “Microslices” are rapid relaxation steps. Liminality is a control knob that changes how freely the system can reorganize.
