Metaphysical lens
Axis, orientation, and inner weather.

IDT can be read as a “physics of meaning”: a living geometry where stability feels like harmony, instability feels like dissonance, and liminality feels like standing at a threshold.

Axis mundi as stabilizer

Many traditions describe an inner axis: a centerline that holds orientation while experience changes. In IDT terms, the axis corresponds to low-variance structure in Z (parts of the state that stay stable across contexts).

Strong axis: slices rotate without shattering identity. Weak axis: small shocks trigger full reconfiguration.

Harmony and dissonance

Harmony is not moral goodness here. It's constraint satisfaction. Dissonance is constraint conflict. When predictions fail, values conflict, or goals contradict actions, dissonance rises.

Metaphysically: “wrongness” in the fabric of experience. Geometrically: energy stays high.

Liminality as threshold space

Rites, trance, grief, awe, psychedelics, and deep focus all resemble liminal states. In IDT, liminality is a control parameter that increases how widely Z can move while searching for a lower-energy configuration.

Meaning as geometry

Meaning is the pattern of distances and angles inside Z. “A lesson learned” is a lasting bend in that geometry: next time the same situation appears, the downhill path is different.
